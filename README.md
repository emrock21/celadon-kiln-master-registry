# Celadon Kiln Master Registry

This registry documents the regional kiln schools, lineages, and technical traditions of Chinese celadon (青瓷).  
Celadon is a single master tradition with deep regional diversity: clay sources, glaze chemistry, firing temperatures, kiln architecture, and aesthetic philosophy vary dramatically across China.

## Purpose

The goal of this contract is to preserve authentic celadon knowledge in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship‑resistant format.  
Each entry records a real kiln site, lineage, or regional school, including:

- region  
- kiln or lineage  
- style name  
- clay source  
- glaze chemistry  
- firing technique  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

Celadon is one of the most important ceramic traditions in East Asia.  
Its regional schools differ as much as Japanese sword‑making or Chinese shadow puppetry:

- Longquan: jade‑green glaze and dragon kilns  
- Yue: earliest high‑quality celadon in China  
- Yaozhou: carved floral motifs under deep olive glaze  
- Ru: sky‑blue imperial ware with sesame‑seed kiln marks  
- Jun: lavender‑blue glaze with purple splashes  
- Ding: ivory‑white glaze with metal rims  

By recording these variations on‑chain, the registry protects cultural memory and supports future research, archaeology, and artistic revival.
